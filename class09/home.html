About X-men

X-Men is an American superhero film series based on the Marvel Comics superhero team of the same name. It was produced by Century Fox and Marvel Entertainment from 2000 to 2020. The X-Men are a team of mutant superheroes in the Marvel Comics universe, created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby in September 1963. They are humans born with a genetic trait known as the "X-gene," which grants them natural superhuman abilities that generally manifest during puberty. Due to their nature, they are often feared and persecuted by society, serving as a metaphor for marginalized groups and tackling themes of racism, prejudice, and social justice
